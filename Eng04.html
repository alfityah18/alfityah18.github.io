The primary factor contributing to the conflict in Palestine today has been intricately explained by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Sunan Abi Dawud 4297 – “al-wahn”. This term defines physical or mental enervation or both, as exemplified in Verse 4 of Surah Maryam and Verse 139 of Surah aal-e-‘Imran. For 500 years, European colonialism has deeply rooted and fostered its way of life in Islamic countries, and this long-term scheme has facilitated the continued colonization of the minds of this ummah even after gaining independence. The way of life that embraces the world and neglects the hereafter is a significant obstruction to the arrival of Allah's help. Nevertheless, the help will indeed come, as promised in Verse 7 of Surah al-Isra, only after we transform the condition of this ummah, echoing the guidance in Verse 11 of Surah ar-Ra’d*. The question is: Do we aspire to pioneer a change in our way of life, or are we content to be remembered in history as the generation of al-wahn?

*Indeed, Allah would never change the condition of a people until they change what is within themselves.
