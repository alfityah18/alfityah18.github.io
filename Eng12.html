1. Shirk

The first nullifier of Islam is shirk; that is, to ascribe partners to Allah (SWT). A common misunderstanding among Muslims is the assumption that shirk refers only to the worship of idols or temple deities. In reality, shirk can manifest in various forms. Broadly speaking, shirk is divided into two types: major shirk (shirk akbar) and minor shirk (shirk asghar). Some scholars add a third type, namely hidden shirk (shirk khafi), though it is in fact included within the second type.

Based on the statement of the Messenger of Allah п·є recorded in Sahih Muslim 86, shirk is the gravest of sins. Through his statement in Sahih al-Bukhari 4974, we come to understand whyвЂ”because it is a sin that affronts the majesty of Allah TaвЂ™ala. Meanwhile, his statement in Sahih al-Bukhari 3334 reminds us of what we have long forgotten: that even when we were still within the loins of Prophet Adam (AS), a covenant was already taken from us by Allah (AWJ) that we should not associate anything with Him.

Know that major shirk expels a person from the fold of Islam; it is not forgiven by Allah if one dies without repenting from it, and one will be punished in the Fire forever. Minor shirk, however, does not invalidate oneвЂ™s Islam, but it nullifies the reward of the deed that contains such shirk, and its perpetrator will not remain in the Fire eternally. In this discussion, we will focus only on several forms of major shirk that commonly occur.

Shirk may occur in three aspects of Tawhid: Rububiyyah, Uluhiyyah, and Asma wa Sifat. Rububiyyah (the aspect of divine lordship) refers to the actions of Allah towards His creationвЂ”such as creating, providing sustenance, and the like. Uluhiyyah (the aspect of worship) refers to the duties of His servants towards Allah, namely singling Him out in all forms of worship or devotionвЂ”such as performing prayer, supplicating, and the like. Asma wa Sifat refers to the Names and Attributes of Allah which He Himself has affirmed for Himself in the Quran and the Sunnah.

Among the forms of shirk in the aspect of divine lordship that exist in the modern age are the belief that: extraterrestrial beings created life across the galaxies, that saints govern the universe, that certain gods or deities provide sustenance, and that natural phenomena possess independent power to bring benefit or harm. Muslims who engage in this type of shirk are in a worse position than the people of Jahiliyyah, because the latter only committed shirk in the aspect of worship.

Among the forms of shirk in the aspect of the Names and Attributes of Allah are the belief that: there exist jinn or unseen beings who possess absolute knowledge of the unseen, and that human figures, such as superheroes, are able to know the future with certainty. What we wish to emphasise are the four forms of shirk most commonly committed by those within this religion itself, namely shirk in the aspect of worship.
