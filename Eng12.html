The call of the preachers referred to by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in this hadith is not a call to minor sins; rather, they are dangers that can undermine one’s creed. This is made clear through the narration of Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman (RA) recorded in Sahih al-Bukhari 7084, in which he said:

كَانَ النَّاسُ يَسْأَلُونَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم عَنِ الْخَيْرِ، وَكُنْتُ أَسْأَلُهُ عَنِ الشَّرِّ، مَخَافَةَ أَنْ يُدْرِكَنِي فَقُلْتُ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ إِنَّا كُنَّا فِي جَاهِلِيَّةٍ وَشَرٍّ فَجَاءَنَا اللَّهُ بِهَذَا الْخَيْرِ، فَهَلْ بَعْدَ هَذَا الْخَيْرِ مِنْ شَرٍّ قَالَ ‏"‏نَعَمْ‏"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ وَهَلْ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ الشَّرِّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ قَالَ ‏"‏نَعَمْ، وَفِيهِ دَخَنٌ‏"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ وَمَا دَخَنُهُ قَالَ ‏"‏قَوْمٌ يَهْدُونَ بِغَيْرِ هَدْىٍ، تَعْرِفُ مِنْهُمْ وَتُنْكِرُ‏"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ فَهَلْ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ الْخَيْرِ مِنْ شَرٍّ قَالَ ‏"‏نَعَمْ، دُعَاةٌ عَلَى أَبْوَابِ جَهَنَّمَ، مَنْ أَجَابَهُمْ إِلَيْهَا قَذَفُوهُ فِيهَا‏"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صِفْهُمْ لَنَا‏.‏ قَالَ ‏"‏هُمْ مِنْ جِلْدَتِنَا، وَيَتَكَلَّمُونَ بِأَلْسِنَتِنَا"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ فَمَا تَأْمُرُنِي إِنْ أَدْرَكَنِي ذَلِكَ قَالَ ‏"‏تَلْزَمُ جَمَاعَةَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ وَإِمَامَهُمْ‏"‏‏.‏ قُلْتُ فَإِنْ لَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُمْ جَمَاعَةٌ وَلاَ إِمَامٌ قَالَ ‏"‏فَاعْتَزِلْ تِلْكَ الْفِرَقَ كُلَّهَا، وَلَوْ أَنْ تَعَضَّ بِأَصْلِ شَجَرَةٍ، حَتَّى يُدْرِكَكَ الْمَوْتُ، وَأَنْتَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ‏"‏‏.‏

People used to ask the Messenger of Allah ﷺ about good, but I used to ask him about evil, fearing that it might reach me. So I said:

“O Messenger of Allah, we were in ignorance and evil, and Allah brought us this good. Will there be any evil after this good?”

He said: “Yes.”

“And will there be any good after that evil?”

“Yes, but it will be tainted.”

“And what is its taint?”

“A people who guide by other than my guidance; you will recognise some things from them and reject others.”

“Then will there be any evil after that good?”

“Yes—callers at the gates of Hell; whoever responds to them, they will throw him into it.”

“O Messenger of Allah, describe them to us.”

“They are from our own people, and they speak with our tongues.”

“What do you command me to do if I reach that time?”

“Adhere to the community of the Muslims and their leader.”

“And if they have no community and no leader?”

“Then keep away from all those groups, even if you must cling to the root of a tree until death reaches you while you are in that state.”

Sahih al-Bukhari 7084 does not present a fixed historical timeline, but rather a recurring pattern that continues to emerge throughout the ages. Ignorance and evil refer to the period of Jahiliyyah before the advent of the Prophet ﷺ. While the good signifies Islam, which united the ummah upon the true guidance brought by him.

Thereafter, evil re-emerged—beginning during the time of the Companions—in the form of political turmoil, internal division, and the rise of deviant factions. This was followed by a phase in which good still remained, yet became intermixed with elements of deviation, misleading leadership, and guidance that was not fully aligned with the Sunnah of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and the Sunnah of the Companions (RA).

Thereafter, from this emerge the preachers who call to various forms of destruction—endangering the creed and potentially causing a person to be cast into the Fire, whether for a very long time, or even eternally. They come from within this very religion and speak with the language of the faithful. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ commanded us not to accept any of their teachings, even if one must remain alone until the end of life. All the more so in this borderless digital world, where anyone can appear on our screens bringing danger.

And the gravest danger is the sins that nullify Islam. There are many matters that can cause a person to fall into apostasy; however, we will confine ourselves to discussing the ten nullifiers of Islam that are most perilous, for many Muslims remain unaware of them and do not fully comprehend their reality. Learn them well, so that you are not deceived and may distance yourself from those who promote them.

1. Shirk

إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَغۡفِرُ أَن يُشۡرَكَ بِهِۦ وَيَغۡفِرُ مَا دُونَ ذَٰلِكَ لِمَن يَشَآءُۚ وَمَن يُشۡرِكۡ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدِ ٱفۡتَرَىٰٓ إِثۡمًا عَظِيمًا

Indeed, Allah does not forgive associating others with Him, but forgives anything else of whoever He wills. And whoever associates others with Allah has indeed committed a tremendous sin.

Surah an-Nisa 4 : Verse 48

وَمَن يُشۡرِكۡ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدۡ ضَلَّ ضَلَٰلَۢا بَعِيدًا

And whoever associates others with Allah has clearly gone far astray.

Surah an-Nisa 4 : Verse 116

إِنَّهُۥ مَن يُشۡرِكۡ بِٱللَّهِ فَقَدۡ حَرَّمَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيۡهِ ٱلۡجَنَّةَ وَمَأۡوَىٰهُ ٱلنَّارُۖ وَمَا لِلظَّٰلِمِينَ مِنۡ أَنصَارࣲ

Whoever associates others with Allah will surely be forbidden Paradise by Allah. Their eternal home will be the Fire. And the wrongdoers will have no helpers.

Surah al-Maidah 5 : Verse 72

The first nullifier of Islam is shirk; that is, to ascribe partners to Allah (SWT). A common misunderstanding among Muslims is the assumption that shirk refers only to the worship of idols or temple deities. In reality, shirk can manifest in various forms. Broadly speaking, shirk is divided into two types: major shirk (shirk akbar) and minor shirk (shirk asghar). Some scholars add a third type, namely hidden shirk (shirk khafi), though it is in fact included within the second type.

Based on the statement of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recorded in Sahih Muslim 86, shirk is the gravest of sins. Through his statement in Sahih al-Bukhari 4974, we come to understand why—because it is a sin that affronts the majesty of Allah Ta’ala. Meanwhile, his statement in Sahih al-Bukhari 3334 reminds us of what we have long forgotten: that even when we were still within the loins of Prophet Adam (AS), a covenant was already taken from us by Allah (AWJ) that we should not associate anything with Him.

Know that major shirk expels a person from the fold of Islam; it is not forgiven by Allah if one dies without repenting from it, and one will be punished in the Fire forever. Minor shirk, however, does not invalidate one’s Islam, but it nullifies the reward of the deed that contains such shirk, and its perpetrator will not remain in the Fire eternally. In this discussion, we will focus only on several forms of major shirk that commonly occur.

Shirk may occur in three aspects of Tawhid: Rububiyyah, Uluhiyyah, and Asma wa Sifat. Rububiyyah (the aspect of divine lordship) refers to the actions of Allah towards His creation—such as creating, providing sustenance, and the like. Uluhiyyah (the aspect of worship) refers to the duties of His servants towards Allah, namely singling Him out in all forms of worship or devotion—such as performing prayer, supplicating, and the like. Asma wa Sifat refers to the Names and Attributes of Allah which He Himself has affirmed for Himself in the Quran and the Sunnah.

Among the forms of shirk in the aspect of divine lordship that exist in the modern age are the belief that: extraterrestrial beings created life across the galaxies, that saints govern the universe, that certain gods or deities provide sustenance, and that natural phenomena possess independent power to bring benefit or harm. Muslims who engage in this type of shirk are in a worse position than the people of Jahiliyyah, because the latter only committed shirk in the aspect of worship.

Among the forms of shirk in the aspect of the Names and Attributes of Allah are the belief that: there exist jinn or unseen beings who possess absolute knowledge of the unseen, and that human figures, such as superheroes, are able to know the future with certainty. What we wish to emphasise are the four forms of shirk most commonly committed by those within this religion itself, namely shirk in the aspect of worship.

(a) Shirk in Love

وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَتَّخِذُ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ أَندَادࣰا يُحِبُّونَهُمۡ كَحُبِّ ٱللَّهِۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ أَشَدُّ حُبࣰّا لِّلَّهِۗ

And among mankind are those who take others besides Allah as equals to Him; they love them as they should love Allah. But those who believe are stronger in their love for Allah.

Surah al-Baqarah 2 : Verse 165

The first form of major shirk in the aspect of worship is shirk in love. Tawhid is to love Allah, to love what Allah loves (such as loving Tawhid and the Sunnah), and to love for the sake of Allah (such as loving a person because he loves Tawhid and the Sunnah). However, when a servant loves a creation as he loves Allah, or even more—devoting to it a reverence equal to that due to God, or to the extent that he is willing to disobey Allah for the sake of that love—he has associated partners with Allah.

Many Muslims wonder: how can a person come to worship idols? Man is not merely a physical body; he possesses a soul and emotions. Emotions are bait used by Satan to ensnare mankind, such that when love overcomes revelation and sound judgement, the victim has already fallen into his grasp. This was the strategy of Iblis against the first community to associate partners with Allah, before the time of Prophet Noah (AS)—an excessive love for the righteous men named Wadd, Suwa’, Yaghuth, Ya’uq, and Nasr—as recorded in Verse 23 of Surah Nuh. From love came memorial statues, and from statues came idols.

And just as it began, idols are not merely carvings. Rather, they are representations of figures who are loved and worshipped, whether righteous people who have passed away, jinn and devils, or mythical characters who are elevated to a status comparable to God. Idols are not necessarily just carved objects; they can also serve as the dwelling place of beings that are worshipped, such as trees, stones, and graves. And the beings that are worshipped are not necessarily dead; some are still alive, such as Pharaoh, who demanded worship for himself.

Such love is truly not worth it, for it will end in a regret without end. That is what will befall those who associate partners with Allah in love, as stated in Verses 97 and 98 of Surah ash-Shu’ara:

تَٱللَّهِ إِن كُنَّا لَفِي ضَلَٰلࣲ مُّبِينٍ

“By Allah! We were clearly mistaken,”

إِذۡ نُسَوِّيكُم بِرَبِّ ٱلۡعَٰلَمِينَ

“When we made you equal to the Lord of all worlds.”

(b) Shirk in Obedience

ٱتَّخَذُوٓاْ أَحۡبَارَهُمۡ وَرُهۡبَٰنَهُمۡ أَرۡبَابࣰا مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱلۡمَسِيحَ ٱبۡنَ مَرۡيَمَ وَمَآ أُمِرُوٓاْ إِلَّا لِيَعۡبُدُوٓاْ إِلَٰهࣰا وَٰحِدࣰاۖ لَّآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَۚ سُبۡحَٰنَهُۥ عَمَّا يُشۡرِكُونَ

They have taken their rabbis and monks, as well as the Messiah, son of Mary, as lords besides Allah, even though they were commanded to worship none but One God. There is no god worthy of worship except Him. Glorified is He above what they associate with Him!

Surah at-Taubah 9 : Verse 31

The second form of major shirk in the aspect of worship is shirk in obedience. Through a hasan hadith narrated by ‘Adi ibn Hatim (RA), recorded in Mu’jam al-Kabir 17/92, when he heard Prophet Muhammad ﷺ reciting that Verse, he—at that time still a Christian—said:

"إِنَّا لَسْنَا نَعْبُدُهُمْ." فَقَالَ: "أَلَيْسَ يُحَرِّمُونَ مَا أَحَلَّ اللَّهُ فَتُحَرِّمُونَهُ، وَيُحِلُّونَ مَا حَرَّمَ اللَّهُ فَتَسْتَحِلُّونَهُ؟" فَقُلْتُ: "بَلَى." فَقَالَ: "فَتِلْكَ عِبَادَتُهُمْ."

“We do not worship them.”

He replied:

“Do they not forbid what Allah has made lawful, so that you also forbid it? And do they not permit what Allah has forbidden, so that you also permit it?”

“Indeed, they do.”

“Then that is their worship.”

These two texts teach us that the word “worship” carries a broader meaning. It is not merely the physical act of prostrating to idols, but also includes the submission of the intellect, the heart, and the emotions to “taghut”—that is, anyone or anything that is adored, followed, and obeyed to the extent that it exceeds the proper bounds.

Allah, Glorified and Exalted be He, cites the scholars of the People of the Book in this context as the prime example of betrayal: they manipulated the laws of Allah to suit their desires, fully aware of what they were doing. Should this occur among the religious figures of this faith, then: whoever follows a person, knowing that that individual forbids what Allah has permitted and permits what Allah has forbidden, and believes that he has the authority to legislate Allah’s laws—has in effect taken that person as an object of worship besides Allah.

(c) Shirk in Intention and Will

مَن كَانَ يُرِيدُ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةَ ٱلدُّنۡيَا وَزِينَتَهَا نُوَفِّ إِلَيۡهِمۡ أَعۡمَٰلَهُمۡ فِيهَا وَهُمۡ فِيهَا لَا يُبۡخَسُونَ

Whoever desires this worldly life and its adornments, We will pay them in full for their deeds in this life—and nothing will be left out.

أُوْلَٰٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَيۡسَ لَهُمۡ فِي ٱلۡأٓخِرَةِ إِلَّا ٱلنَّارُۖ وَحَبِطَ مَا صَنَعُواْ فِيهَا وَبَٰطِلࣱ مَّا كَانُواْ يَعۡمَلُونَ

Those are the ones for whom there will be nothing in the Hereafter except the Fire. What they did in this world will be lost, and what they used to do will be worthless.

Surah Hud 11 : Verses 15 & 16

The third form of major shirk in the aspect of worship is shirk in intention and will. Although it relates to niyyah, riya—that is, showing off one’s deeds or acts of goodness in order to gain the praise or admiration of others—when it occurs only occasionally, and not purely for the sake of people, does not constitute major shirk that nullifies one’s Islam. To understand this form of shirk more clearly, we must consider the two earlier Verses together with Verses 162 and 163 of Surah al-An‘am:

قُلۡ إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحۡيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلۡعَٰلَمِينَ

Say (O Prophet), “Surely my prayer, my worship, my life, and my death are all for Allah—Lord of all worlds.”

لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُۥۖ وَبِذَٰلِكَ أُمِرۡتُ وَأَنَا۠ أَوَّلُ ٱلۡمُسۡلِمِينَ

“He has no partner. So I am commanded, and so I am the first to submit.”

These two Verses remind us that intention does not apply only to acts of worship; rather, it encompasses one’s entire life, even one’s death. Whoever makes the true purpose of all their righteous deeds, their life, and their death for other than Allah, and seeks neither His reward nor the meeting with Him—then they have committed major shirk in intention and will. This form of shirk also includes deeds which, even if performed only occasionally, are directed to other than Allah; such as slaughtering an animal for a saint or a jinn in order to obtain benefit or avert harm.

Verses 15 and 16 of Surah Hud not only introduce a more subtle form of shirk, but also expand the meaning of taghut—which is not confined to objects such as idols, jinn, and human beings, but also encompasses those things that are pursued, such as worldly life and all its adornments. This includes power, wealth, and name—when these are made the ultimate object of intention and will, such that Allah is entirely absent from one’s purpose in life and ultimate aim for the Hereafter—it becomes major shirk.

(d) Shirk in Supplication

لَهُۥ دَعۡوَةُ ٱلۡحَقِّۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ يَدۡعُونَ مِن دُونِهِۦ لَا يَسۡتَجِيبُونَ لَهُم بِشَيۡءٍ إِلَّا كَبَٰسِطِ كَفَّيۡهِ إِلَى ٱلۡمَآءِ لِيَبۡلُغَ فَاهُ وَمَا هُوَ بِبَٰلِغِهِۦۚ وَمَا دُعَآءُ ٱلۡكَٰفِرِينَ إِلَّا فِي ضَلَٰلࣲ

True supplication is to Him alone. Those whom they call upon besides Him do not respond to them at all; except like one who stretches out his hands toward water so that it may reach his mouth, but it never reaches it. And the supplication of the disbelievers is nothing but in misguidance.

Surah ar-Ra’d 13 : Verse 14

The fourth form of major shirk in the aspect of worship is shirk in supplication. As the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said in Jami’ at-Tirmidhi 3372, du‘a is an act of worship. Therefore, supplication must not be directed to anyone other than Allah—whether to a jinn behind an idol, a saint in his grave, a highly venerated Prophet such as Jesus (AS), or anyone or anything else. This is because only Allah has the power to grant what is requested. Those who go against this are not merely associating partners with Allah; they are also engaging in futility—like a thirsty person reaching out for water, only to find it was a mirage.

Among the well-known confusions or distortions in this matter is istighathah, which refers to seeking help or rescue from other than Allah in matters that are solely under the power of Allah (AWJ). If what is claimed by those who permit it were true, there would certainly be a Verse from the Quran or an authentic hadith from the Prophet ﷺ to support it. Yet there is none. What exists instead are clear and unequivocal prohibitions, such as in Verse 65 of Surah al-‘Ankabut:

فَإِذَا رَكِبُواْ فِي ٱلۡفُلۡكِ دَعَوُاْ ٱللَّهَ مُخۡلِصِينَ لَهُ ٱلدِّينَ فَلَمَّا نَجَّىٰهُمۡ إِلَى ٱلۡبَرِّ إِذَا هُمۡ يُشۡرِكُونَ

When they embark on a ship (caught in a storm), they cry out to Allah alone in sincere devotion. But as soon as He delivers them safely to land, at once they return to associating others with Him.

The most convoluted confusion and the most misleading distortion in the matter of supplication lies in tawassul. Those who have gone astray use “tawassul” merely as a term, whereas what they actually do is supplicating to created beings or taking them as intermediaries. In reality, both of these acts constitute major shirk. In addition to errors in understanding its meaning in Classical Arabic, what is taking place is what is mentioned by the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in the hasan hadith narrated by ‘Uqbah bin ‘Amir (RA) in Musnad Ahmad 17421: his concern regarding those who distort the meaning of the Verses of the Quran.

The meaning of “tawassul” or “wasilah” is already well established in Arabic lexicons, both classical and contemporary, and cannot be subject to distortion. The term appears only twice in the Quran. Verse 35 of Surah al-Maidah conveys its general meaning as “a means or way of drawing closer” to Allah, referring to all forms of righteous deeds that are pleasing to Him—namely those taught by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Meanwhile, Verse 57 of Surah al-Isra corrects the misunderstanding of the term “wasilah” in the context of supplication, rejecting the notion that it means calling upon, seeking from, or asking help from the awliya of Allah, or taking them as intermediaries.

In the context of supplication, “wasilah” refers to a means or way by which one seeks Allah’s acceptance of a prayer. It must be understood as a complement to supplication—not the supplication itself, nor the taking of intermediaries. It must adhere to the methods demonstrated by The Chosen One ﷺ and his Companions (RA). Among the permissible forms of tawassul during supplication are invoking the Most Beautiful Names and Attributes of Allah, sending blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ, and mentioning one’s righteous deeds.

For some, this issue may seem confusing. However, the key lies in distinguishing between major shirk—namely, calling upon, seeking from, or asking help from other than Allah—and the prescribed form of tawassul, which is to employ permissible means by which Allah grants acceptance to supplication. As stated in Verse 14 of Surah ar-Ra’d at the beginning of this section: true supplication is to Him alone. Likewise, as the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said in Jami’ at-Tirmidhi 2516: if you ask, ask of Allah; and if you seek help, seek help from Allah.

2. Intermediary

أَلَا لِلَّهِ ٱلدِّينُ ٱلۡخَالِصُۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّخَذُواْ مِن دُونِهِۦٓ أَوۡلِيَآءَ مَا نَعۡبُدُهُمۡ إِلَّا لِيُقَرِّبُونَآ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ زُلۡفَىٰٓ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَحۡكُمُ بَيۡنَهُمۡ فِي مَا هُمۡ فِيهِ يَخۡتَلِفُونَۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهۡدِي مَنۡ هُوَ كَٰذِبࣱ كَفَّارࣱ
