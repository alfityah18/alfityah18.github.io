The Signs Have Been Realized

In 1966, a collaboration between Patrick Steptoe, a gynecologist & Sir Robert Edwards, an embryologist from Britain, gave rise to the revolutionary technology of in vitro fertilisation (IVF). This pioneering feat was first witnessed in the birth of Louise Brown, the world's first test-tube baby, on July 25, 1978. Yet, this success also paved the way for a more controversial procedure. On August 1, 1985, Shannon Boff, a housewife from the USA, became the first surrogate mother to receive remuneration for carrying the sperm and egg of another couple, in effect becoming a servant who gave birth to her master or mistress...
